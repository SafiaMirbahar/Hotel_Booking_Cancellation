# Hotel_Booking_Cancellation
Hotel Booking Cancellation Analysis
📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes hotel booking data to understand the key factors influencing reservation cancellations for City Hotel and Resort Hotel. High cancellation rates negatively impact revenue and room utilization, making it essential to identify patterns and actionable solutions.

Using real-world booking data from 2015 to 2017, the analysis focuses on uncovering trends related to pricing, seasonality, booking channels, and customer behavior.

🎯 Business Problem

Hotels face significant revenue loss and underutilized rooms due to frequent booking cancellations.
The objective of this project is to:

Identify factors that lead to higher cancellation rates

Quantify the revenue impact of canceled bookings

Provide data-driven recommendations to reduce cancellations and improve occupancy

❓ Research Questions

What factors influence hotel booking cancellations?

How do pricing and seasonal trends affect cancellations?

Which booking channels contribute most to cancellations?

How do cancellations impact hotel revenue and room occupancy?

📊 Key Insights

City Hotel experienced a higher cancellation rate (43%) compared to Resort Hotel (28%).

Cancellations peak during February and June, while August and December show the lowest rates.

Canceled bookings have a higher average daily rate (ADR) than non-canceled bookings, indicating price sensitivity.

Certain booking channels and countries contribute disproportionately to cancellations.

Significant revenue loss is observed during high-demand months due to canceled reservations.

🛠 Tools & Technologies

Python

Pandas & NumPy – Data cleaning and preprocessing

Matplotlib & Seaborn – Data visualization

Excel – Summary analysis and reporting

📌 Recommendations

Apply flexible pricing strategies during high-cancellation months.

Improve booking management with reminders and easy rescheduling options.

Target marketing efforts toward high-risk segments and booking channels.

Encourage early confirmations through discounts or added benefits.

✅ Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates how pricing, seasonality, and booking behavior significantly affect hotel cancellations. By applying data-driven strategies, hotels can reduce cancellations, improve room utilization, and increase overall revenue.
